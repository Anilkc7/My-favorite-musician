My Favourite Musician: Anne Marie

Introduction

Anne-Marie Nicholson, known professionally as Anne-Marie, is an English singer, songwriter, and musician. She was born on April 7, 1991, in East Tilbury, Essex, England. Anne-Marie began her career in music as a member of the UK dance group Rudimental, providing vocals on their hit single "Rumour Mill" in 2015. She gained wider recognition in 2016 with her solo debut single "Alarm", which reached the top 20 in the UK charts. Since then, Anne-Marie has released a string of successful singles, including "Ciao Adios," "Friends" and "2002", and has collaborated with artists such as Marshmello, David Guetta, and Clean Bandit. Her debut studio album, "Speak Your Mind," was released in 2018 and received critical acclaim. Anne-Marie is known for her powerful voice, catchy pop hooks, and honest, relatable lyrics.

Biography

Anne Marie was born on April 7, 1991 in East Tilbury, Essex, England. She began singing at a young age and was a member of a musical theatre company before pursuing a solo career. In 2013, she auditioned for the ninth series of the British singing competition show, The X Factor, but was eliminated in the early stages.

Despite this setback, Anne Marie continued to work on her music and signed a record deal with Asylum Records in 2015. Her debut EP, Karate, was released in 2015 and featured the hit single "Alarm." She followed up with the 2017 EP, Ciao Adios, which included the popular songs "Ciao Adios" and "Heavy."

Her debut studio album, Speak Your Mind, was released in 2018 and featured the hit single "Friends" with DJ Marshmello. The album was a commercial success and earned Anne Marie several award nominations, including four at the 2019 Brit Awards.
